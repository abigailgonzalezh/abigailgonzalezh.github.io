About Me

Hi! I'm Abigail, a passionate student of B.S. Computer Science and Technology at Instituto Tecnológico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey in Mexico. I have knowledge in object-oriented programming, web development, data structure and interactive design. I became interested in technology when I was a child, thanks to video games since then I have been passionate about learning subjects related to technology.

Projects

Naval Battle

Development of a game simulator that uses the naval battle bases, allowing the user to compete against an artificial intelligence machine or against another player. You can start a new game, load a saved game and exit whenever you want. The game was developed with Java.

Videogame Storage System

This system helps you store your video games by genre like sports, RPG, action, shooters, etc. It lets you insert, delete, show, arrange, search, safe, favorite, delete from favorites, show favorites and exit the program. This system was developed with Java.

Magic Moments (Hackaton)

Magic Moments is an iOs application prototype that allows people with different disabilites to find restaurants, services and leisure activities that are tailored for their needs. For example, if a person with blindless is looking for a restaurant this app would show them different restaurants, that are nearby, that have braille menus. This prototype was made with JustInMind.

Life

Life is a video game set in a post-apocalyptical world where the protagonist is a little girl named Lucy who tries to fix everything by collecting orbs that eventually will grant them the opportunity to fix earth.

Hilfe (Hackaton)

Hilfe is the name of a website that serves as a memorial for people who have been killed, kidnapped or disappeared in Mexico. This website was made like an artistic protest for all the insecurity problems that Mexico is having now, we wanted to make a memorial for people to not forget about the problems we have in this country. This website was made with JustInMind.

TecRide

Prototype design of a mobile application that allows the students of the Tecnológico de Monterrey, Campus Puebla to acquire a transportation service given by another student. The student that gave the transportation service would obtain a reward like coupons for the cafeteria or social service hours. This prototype was developed with iRise.
